# Tidy Up

> The **second game** on `merge_core` — the cozy "merge-to-restore" engine themed as **decluttering & organizing**. Where the Grove *grows* a world, Tidy Up *sorts* one: you merge clutter into order and turn chaotic, messy spaces into clean, calm, beautiful ones. This doc carries only Tidy Up's specifics and references the core pattern each instantiates. Read alongside `../core/merge_spec.md`.

## 1 · The Tidy Up Fantasy

*Instantiates Core §1 (Concept, Pillars & Core Loop) and Core §7 (giver narrative arcs).*

**Tidy Up** dresses the core merge-to-restore loop as **decluttering**: the player rummages cluttered spaces, **merges loose stuff into sorted, put-away order** ("everything in its bucket"), and **visibly restores** a chaotic, messy home into a clean, calm, lovely one. The hook is the **deep, low-stakes satisfaction of sorting like-with-like** plus the **before→after** of a tidied room — for the very large audience that finds organizing, cleaning, and decluttering soothing (the *Unpacking* / tidying-ASMR / "does it spark joy" pleasure).

> **One-sentence fantasy:** one persistent merge board where you pull clutter from messy piles, merge like-with-like up a **tidiness ladder** into neatly stowed things, deliver them to the people whose space it is, and **watch a chaotic home become calm, clean, and something to be proud of.**

The core reframe **"merging is building"** instantiates here as **"merging is *tidying*."** The grove grows; Tidy Up *sorts*. The noun-map:

| Core abstraction | Tidy Up instance |
|---|---|
| working clearing (board) | a **cluttered surface** — a messy floor, a junk drawer, a moving-day heap |
| themed resource (energy) | **Pep** — your tidying get-up-and-go; refills with rest / a cuppa (the daily "tea-break" free refill) *(name OPEN, §4)* |
| theme objects that emit lines (generators) | **clutter piles / boxes** you rummage — tap to pull out the next thing to sort |
| growth/production lines | **categories of stuff** (Books · Clothes · Dishes · Toys …) each merging **loose → sorted → stowed** (a sock → a pair → a folded stack → a filled drawer) *(set OPEN, §4)* |
| themed obstacles | **mess / grime / clutter-drifts** that wall off the space — cleared as you tidy outward |
| themed quest-givers | **the residents** whose space it is — each with a tidying request and a small story their stuff tells |
| the spend surface | the **home** — rooms restored **messy → tidy** (the before→after) |
| ambient life | a **pet** (cat / dog) pottering, dust-motes settling, light warming as the room calms *(OPEN)* |

**Pillars carry from Core**, and Tidy Up adds its own flavour: **Satisfaction** (the *click* of a thing finally put away — the genre's whole reason for being) and **Calm** (resentment lives only on Pep; everything else soothes). **Visible progress** instantiates as **Visible tidying** — the home de-clutters in place.

## 2 · The Story (PROPOSED — confirm or redirect, §4)

A **wordless**, grounded-cozy story (the *No-required-reading* pillar holds — told in faces, objects, and before→after, never required prose). Deliberately **distinct from the Grove's overt spirit-world**: here the magic is in the **satisfaction and the memory held in people's things**.

**Premise (proposed).** You inherit — or move into — a **cluttered home left full of a life's belongings** (a grandparent's house is the working idea). Sorting it room by room, you **clear the chaos and, through the objects, quietly piece together whose life this was** — a wordless story told in what they kept. Word spreads that you have the gift, and you become the **neighbourhood's go-to organizer**, helping others declutter their spaces (and, a little, their lives).

**The architecture (same engine logic as the grove, built to extend):**

- **Zone = a room** — a tidying beat (a room restored = one before→after reveal).
- **Map = a home / space** — a self-contained **episode**: one resident, their clutter, **one keepsake-object or room that carries the heart**, and a real resolution.
- **Spine = personal first, then others.** **Map 1 is your own (inherited) home** — emotional, and it teaches the loop; the payoff is *discovering the story in the stuff* and making the place yours. Then **each later map is someone else's space to help** — an endless, warm parade of homes and the people in them. *(Parallels the grove's "free your family first, then help others.")*
- **The reusable heart-device.** Every home hides **one object or room that holds its story** — sorted/restored last, it delivers the episode's emotional beat (a photo box, a child's drawer, a workshop). The Grove's "someone de-transforms as you restore" device, re-skinned for **objects + memory**.

**The cast (proposed).**

- **You** — the organizer *(a person — identity OPEN, §4; or a small magical "tidy helper" if we want a whisper of Grove-style magic)*.
- **The residents** — the "interesting figures" + quest-givers: a downsizing gran, a frazzled new parent, a kid who can't let go, an artist drowning in their own chaos… each a personality + a tidying need + a story their things tell.
- **A companion** — a pet that potters about and warms the space *(OPEN)*.
- **Map 1's heart** — the person whose home you inherited, met only through their belongings *(OPEN)*.

**Wordless delivery.** Faces and gesture; the **before→after** of each room; the **memory in objects** (a sorted photo box that fans open; a child's drawer that fills); the resident's **wish** read as a picture (an overflowing shelf, a toppling tower). Optional names/flavour ride on top; a non-reader misses nothing required (Core §7).

## 3 · Mechanic mapping (how the engine themes)

Tidy Up is the **unchanged** `merge_core` loop (Core §1) in tidy clothes:

- **The merge = sorting.** Loose like-items combine **up a tidiness ladder** (Core §6 lines) — *loose → grouped → sorted → stowed* — the literal "put it in the same bucket." The **top tier** is "perfectly put away" (a full, neat drawer / shelf / crate) — sold to the merchant or spent on a room-gate (Core §6/§9).
- **The generator = a clutter pile / box** you rummage (Core §6): spend **Pep** to pull out the next item to sort; a burst = grabbing a handful.
- **The givers = residents** with requests (Core §7): "sort the bookshelf" = deliver a stowed *Books* set. Their **arcs** (Core §7) are the per-home stories.
- **The restore surface = the home** (Core §8): rooms go **messy → tidy**; the upgrade → passive-yield loop themes as **a tidied room that stays nice and "pays back"** (a plant grows, the cat naps, the light warms — you collect calm).
- **Obstacles = mess / grime** (Core §4), cleared outward as expansion.
- **Economy, the energy wall, monetization, live-ops, re-engagement** are all the **Core engine** (§4 / §10 / §17 / §18), tuned to the same cozy spine as the grove — gentle, no pay-to-win, opt-in social, recurring-not-predatory scarcity.

The engine is **already built for this** — Tidy Up is a **content + art + naming reskin**, not new engine systems (the only net-new design is the §4 decisions below).
